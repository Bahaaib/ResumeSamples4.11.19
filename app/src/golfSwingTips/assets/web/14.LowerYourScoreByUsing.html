Lower Your Score By Using These 5 Putting Tips

By John Beer

We all want to knock off at least one stroke a hole and so often this can be done on the green.

The beauty of practicing at home is no one will see what you are doing. All of a sudden you will be a star by amazing the others on the green.

A missed putt is frustrating, and we have all done that! Let's see what we can do about it.

One is often tempted to buy a new putter in the hope it will solve the problem. Yes, we know there is a certain buzz when we buy a new club. "Everything will be OK now" and then it is not, simply because the old habits are still there. By all means have your buzz but don't spend any more than $10 and get the putter re gripped. Then, when your fresh habit is working well, that is the time, maybe, to consider a new putter.

If the new found habits are working well you may choose to keep your trusty friend after all.

Having just had a re grip that is modern with a flat surface for the thumbs, my putter feels like new. Goodness knows how long the old grip was on there! The re gripping was done while having a mug of tea and a bun after the last round. I came home with the "new" putter and was looking forward to my next game but in the meantime there was plenty of practice indoors.

The success of these tips will be all the greater if you keep each Tip to a separate day. That way the habit is much more likely to stay in your mind and be of benefit when you are putting for real out on the course.

Great Putting Tip # 1

After hitting the ball quite well you get on the green. Now it's just a matter of getting the ball down into the cup and move on to the next tee. While this may sound easy, given the short distance, there are those who still have a hard time doing so. To help you there are several golf swing drills you can use to reduce your usual number of putts.

The first drill is Right Hand only if you are right-handed. Put your left hand behind your back. Just reverse these instructions if you are a left-hander.

This drill lets you focus on the palm of your right hand as it moves towards the cup. Place ten balls on the carpet or other flat surface and set them out in a line and work your way along all ten. Your target can be say, a cup on its side, or a tin.

Feet firm, hips firm, head still, looking down at the ball. The only movement is the ridged arm plus the putter being moved by the shoulders enough to draw the putter back to pendulum through the ball and finish ahead of where the ball was.

When you do this Right Hand Only drill do be conscious of the toe of the putter in the follow through. Should the toe turn away from the cup this means you are turning your hand. You know you are doing it right when the face of the putter is square to the cup.

Practice this for 15-20 minutes today and establish the habit in your mind before moving on.

Great Putting Tip #2

Previously you perfected putting with your right hand only.

Now, for the right-handed we shall look at using the Left Hand only drill. Put your right hand behind your back. If you are left-handed then reverse this instruction.

Have you remembered the stance? Feet firm, hips firm, head still, looking down at the ball. The only movement is the ridged arm plus the putter being moved by the shoulders enough to draw the putter back to pendulum through the ball and finish ahead of where the ball was.

The emphasis here is for you to concentrate on the back of your left hand which is holding the club with the left hand only. Think really hard about the back of the hand as the club moves the ball towards the cup.

As before be conscious of the toe of the putter in the follow through. Should the toe of the putter turn away from the cup it this means you are turning your hand.

To do it right, the face of the putter needs to be square to the cup.

Use your ten balls and practice this drill for 15-20 minutes to establish the habit in your mind before moving on to the next tip.

We have had the One Handed Drill with the left hand and the One Handed Drill with the right hand.

So before we go on we'll use the two hands for a few minutes. When you use two hands you may find you have too much power. If so, it needs adjusting but do keep the posture the same.

The stance is all important and bears restating. Feet firm, hips firm, head still, looking down at the ball. The only movement is the ridged arms plus the putter being moved by the shoulders enough to draw the putter back to pendulum through the ball and finish ahead of where the ball was.

Great Putting Tip #3

Now for some more accuracy, this tip came from a professional and you will find it a great help.

Fix up a length of string or tape so you are putting along the line of string and straight into the cup.

The equipment is simple. A suitable length of string, and a pile of books behind the cup and a pile of books to your right as you address the ball. The top books have the string tied round them so it is tight and at a height sufficient to allow the ball and putter underneath. The ball will travel under the string from the putter into the cup. Piles of books could be used or soup tins or anything else which serves the purpose. If you are on a practice green 2 kebab skewers will be good and easily removed without any damage to the surface of the green.

Again, 10 balls and away you go.

When you are putting for real just imagine the string, and make allowances for slopes and the condition of the green.

Great Putting Tip #4

Sometimes there is frustration when the putter is grounded during the putting stroke. To avoid this have a couple of practice swings to ensure the putter is just above the ground then shuffle forward to make the putt.

These two practice swings register in your mind both the weight and direction you consider necessary.

Remember the point about the face of the putter being square to the cup and the stance?

And now an important bit. With the ball in the middle of your stance do not swing the putter back very far because the further back you go the more likely the contact with the ball will be a degree or two out of true. By the time the ball comes to rest it can be some way off target.

The way round this is to have a very short back swing, maybe five inches, (13cm) and punch your way smoothly through the ball by six or seven inches (15 or 18cm). Make sure it is along the line of the imaginary string. This is the same as the follow through when teeing off. The club head is still making straight for the cup which is where your ball is due to arrive.

All the time keep the cup in your mind - draw the putter back, strike through the ball and on, under the string towards the cup. So, accelerate for five inches, connecting with the ball at four inches at your chosen power. The club continues for another seven inches all the time directly towards the cup. And plop! In she goes!

In other words the putter travels in a straight line from five inches back to seven inches beyond the point where the ball was.

Practise this under the string without a ball. Make a mark where the ball would be to get the feel and point of impact right.

The putter is under control, you are under control and you improve your chances of turning in a good score. All of which is much better than hitting and hoping.

Great Putting Tip #5

This last tip builds on all the previous ones and is the icing on the cake. Your putting success rate will be way up and all because of one very simple extra aspect.

When you are on the green you will stretch the imaginary string from the hole to the ball.

And here is the magic. You will identify a point on the ground about three inches in front of the ball. This is directly in line with the cup and is the point over which the ball must pass on its way to success.

Just concentrate on that point on the ground. Do not look at the ball, nor at the club and certainly not at the cup. You are now used to having the head of the putter square to the cup and you know the ball will pass over the selected point just in front of the ball on its way to the cup.

Practice all these aspects, even five minutes twice a day, for several days, and you will significantly improve your success rate. You owe it to yourself. Recently there was an enforced lay off, and on return, the opening score was not a disaster and was greatly helped by the putting. Your overall score can be cut by using these tips on the green.
