6 Extremely Easy Tips to Lose Weight Quickly

By Ben Pickering-Watson

It doesn't have to be difficult to shed those unwanted pounds; you can lose weight. I'm sure you hate the word diet; just thinking about that word probably makes you want to avoid the process all together, right? There are some very effective ways in which you can lose weight effectively. So lets take a look at what it is that you can do to lose weight quickly.

1. Don't make yourself go hungry. The trick to losing weight is to avoid diets altogether. Yes, it is odd sounding, but it's also very true. Actually, although it's unperceptive, many people cannot lose weight simply because they do not eat enough food throughout their day. You might feel pleased to see that you are shedding that extra fat on your thighs and stomach by going without food. If this is the case, then you must be unaware of the fact that these pounds will start piling back on very quickly. Why? Inadequate food intake will result in an intolerable lack of fuel for the body. Eventually, your body will figure out that it is not getting enough energy for survival. Your body will feel threatened and will gain weight as a defense mechanism. Humans' bodies have evolved to protect us as much as possible and our bodies fight our own selves to keep us living. The first thing that people think when they want to lose weight is that they have to consume much fewer calories. However, this actually causes the exact opposite of the purpose. I recommended your daily calorie intake be reduced by no more than 500 calories.

2. Eating more often throughout your day. Make sure that the portions are much smaller has been proven to increase your metabolism, use more calories and will make you feel more energetic. It is preferable to eat five or six smaller meals throughout the day rather than the traditional three square meals. Following this practice will help maintain a high metabolic rate during the entire day. Consuming small frequent meals can satisfy your appetite while suppressing over- indulgence. Plan on eating something every three hours.

3. Your most important task in the morning is to eat a healthy, nutritious breakfast. More than likely, when you wake up the morning it will have been at least 12 hours since your last meal, which means that by that time, you're body is running low on energy. You need to eat more when your body demands it. Breakfast should be your biggest meal, and your smallest meal should be your dinner. This is completely opposite of the average meal plan experienced by most people - a skimpy breakfast, big lunch and a large dinner. But think about it - your body will use all of the energy from a large breakfast, and the fat will not be stored, as you have fasted all night long. However, since your metabolism slows when you sleep and nighttime activities consume fewer calories, an overindulgent dinner is likely to go straight to your hips. It is more probable that you will store extra carbohydrates as fat when you are fuller after a day of eating.

4. Stay hydrated - most people fail to drink enough water in the course of a day. Adequate water in your body is needed to burn fat and to keep cells healthy and hydrated. Have you ever woken up in the morning feeling so out of it that you'd almost think you were hung over? Dehydration is most likely the cause. Symptoms of dehydration are headaches, tiredness and fatigue. Your body's most plentiful nutrient is water. It contains about 60% - 70% water and your body depends on it for every task it performs in some way. Without water, your body cannot work and it will die. 

5. Try not to eat a lot of sugar. Foods with high sugar content are processed very fast, providing you with a large energy lift, which lasts only a short time. Consuming too many sugary foods will results in a sharp peak in your blood sugar followed by an abrupt rise in your insulin levels. Insulin quickly removes sugar from your blood, which in turn causes your blood sugar to drop below normal. When the sugar levels drop quickly, this causes loss of energy and also causes hunger and cravings for more sugary foods You will eat more sweets to quench your hunger, resulting in an on-going repeat of the energy peak and energy crash cycle. Plan your meals around lots of fruits and vegetables, lean meat and protein rich foods, with a little bread, rice or pasta for that carbohydrate fix you call for. Of course, you can't go the rest of your life without any sugar, but sugary foods and drinks should only be eaten every so often.

6. Some easy ways to exercise are to walk instead of taking the car if you're only going a short distance, to take the stairs instead of taking the elevator, or to go out for a jog, a bike ride, or to skate. If working out at the gym is not for you, use these activities and other chores around the house to give yourself a good workout. If you do this consistently, you will lose that excess weight without even noticing the work that you're doing with these simple activities. Burning fat through exercise is better for your metabolism than just eating less. This way will burn off calories without lowering your metabolic rate. The best way to burn fat is aerobic exercise. In fact, while exercising, fat will turn into fuel for your body. Make an effort to include a half hour or more of light exercise in your daily routine.

To summarize, in order to lose weight quickly, you must reduce your daily caloric intake by no more than 500 calories, reduce your intake of sugar, eat smaller meals more often throughout the day, eat a balanced breakfast, drink lots of water, and exercise. Use these guidelines and you will achieve your ideal weight in a minimum amount of time.
