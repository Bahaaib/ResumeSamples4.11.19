Concerns On Teeth Whitening Procedures

Pearly white teeth make a person’s smile attractive. But if yours are discolored you may hesitate on flashing your smile. Thankfully, that can now be solved with teeth-whitening treatments. You can choose among many different ways in achieving that perfect smile. For one, you can decide to opt for a professional teeth whitening treatment or use drugstore solutions.

But is one better than the other?

Decide on the procedure you want while taking into consideration, the severity of your teeth discoloration, amount you are willing to spend, and the results you want. Yellowish teeth will probably lighten more than brownish or grayish ones. Here’s how to choose the right tooth-whitening treatment for you.

Option No. 1: Laser Bleaching at the Dentist's Office. With laser bleaching, the gum tissue is protected then a strong bleach is applied your teeth. The bleach is activated with the use of a laser light to make it work. . The bleach used in this treatment is a stronger solution than the one you can use at home wherein you don’t have a dentist’s assistance and your gums are left unprotected. This treatment consists of three bleaching treatments that each takes 20 minutes so you can have your desired bright smile within an hour. Of all teeth-whitening options, the laser bleaching costs the most.

Option No. 2: Professional Bleaching Trays. Your dentist may also offer a treatment using bleaching trays wherein bleaching trays that perfectly fit your teeth will be fashioned out of the impression of your teeth. You will use a special bleaching solution in these trays every day at home for a week or two. When the discoloration is really bad or the results you want are more dramatic, you can use bleaching trays after getting your laser bleaching treatment.

Option No. 3: Over-The-Counter Teeth-Whitening Kits. There are many types of teeth-whitening products that you can buy in drugstores. Kits to make your own trays, gels, swabs, strips, paints are now available. Strips seem to be the best choice because you're less likely to swallow bleach or harm your gums with them. These over-the-counter solutions are not strong enough for severe discoloration but they can be used by people minor discoloration especially among young ones with their enamel still strong and less susceptible to staining.

Before finally getting that decision and undergoing a teeth-whitening treatment, take these into consideration. First there’s the cost which should be well-suited to your budget. These days, the cost of professional teeth-whitening treatments depends on the option you choose but they usually cost $500 to $1,200. On the other hand, the price of at-home kits ranges from $15 to $50.

You should also consider the post-teeth whitening maintenance.

As it is, results from teeth-whitening treatments are not for life, and those who get them need to get touch-ups once in a while. Results for in-office bleaching can last up to a year, at-home professional whitening trays last 6 to 12 months, and 3-6 months for over-the-counter whitening products. The lifestyle of the person also can affect the duration of results. Your results will fade faster if you smoke and regularly consume food and drinks that can stain teeth, such as coffee, tea, and blueberries. So if drink coffee three times a day, you have to visit the dentist in six months for a touch-up. You can go one to two years between treatments without touch-ups if you avoid the mentioned habits and brush your teeth regularly.

Another important thing to consider would be the risks teeth whitening could render to your overall dental health. The process is known to make teeth sensitive to hot and cold afterwards. It usually doesn’t last long but it can be quite uncomfortable. Thee gums and other soft tissues inside your mouth can also be harmed by the bleach, causing mouth sores and discomfort, so you must make sure that the bleaching material do not touch these tissues.

To fully understand such considerations, discuss with your dentist the condition of your teeth so you could get the best advice. Apart from that, you can consult on which treatment to choose without compromising your dental health.
