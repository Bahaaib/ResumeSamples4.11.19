Herby Tuna Steak

Here is a quick and easy recipe using tuna steak that can be grilled, broiled or even baked. This gives a single serving but you can just double or triple the measures if you wan to make more. You can mix up the herbs with different ones or just use the ones below. Use equal amounts of the suggested herbs.

Shopping List:

1 six oz uncooked tuna steak

 teaspoon dried rosemary

 teaspoon dried oregano

 teaspoon dried marjoram

 teaspoon dried thyme

Balsamic vinegar

Small pinch sea salt

Freshly ground black pepper

Preparation:

Defrost your fish if frozen.

Preheat the oven to 400.

Rinse the tuna steak with water and pat dry with kitchen towels.

Prepare a seasoning mix by mixing together equal parts of dried oregano, marjoram, rosemary and thyme. Cover the bottom of a baking dish with balsamic vinegar and gently mix in the herbs.

Place the fish into the baking dish and allow it to soak up the mixture for at least and 30 minutes in the fridge. Make sure the mixture goes all over the fish.

Bake for 25 or 30 minutes on a rack in the middle of the oven.

Season with salt and pepper when cooked.

Serve with a selection of cooked vegetables and you have a yummy and healthy meal.

Potato Gnocchi and Roasted Capsicum in Tomato Sauce

A delicious, low fat, high protein meal. It is ideal for dinner or lunch.

Shopping List:

600g gnocchi

2 cloves garlic

200g cherry tomatoes, halved

80g snake beans chopped

80g roasted chopped capsicum

100g button mushrooms

80g Fetta crumbled

Small handful of Basil

Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation:

First, chop the garlic and tear the basil. Slice the mushrooms finely.

Cook the tomatoes in a small saucepan over a medium heat until they are just softened. Then, add the roasted capsicum and stir the two together.

Once this is done, take the garlic and mushrooms and add them to the pan. Simmer for 7 to 10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Quick Fish

Not eating fish anymore? Lots of us are not. Make a start with this fabulously easy haddock recipe.

Shopping List

4 haddock fillets or any decent white fish

1 tomato

1 cup parsley

1 cup shallots

1 garlic clove

1 lemons

Preparation:

Chop the tomato, parsley, shallots and the garlic.

Add a little olive oil to a frying pan and add the parsley along with the vegetables. Squeeze some lemon over the top of them

Lightly add a little olive oil onto a baking dish and add the fish to it. Bake the fish in the oven following the instructions given on the packet or by your fish monger.

Gently saut the vegetables while the fish are cooking.

Serve the fish as soon as cooked and gently spread the vegetable mixture over them. Eat straight away. A quick and easy lunch or dinner.
